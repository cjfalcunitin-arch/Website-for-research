Factors Influencing the Non-Adoption of Online Payment Systems Among Sari-Sari Store Owners in Malabon City

Abstract

The increasing adoption of technology has transformed how numerous businesses manage financial transactions. Online payment platforms, including e-wallets, internet banking, and QR code payments, provide convenience, speed, and security for both businesses and their customers. Despite these advantages and the promotion by government bodies and financial institutions in the Philippines, many sari-sari store owners in Malabon City continue to rely on conventional cash dealings.

This research aims to identify and analyze the factors influencing the non-adoption of online payment systems among sari-sari store owners in Malabon City. Through comprehensive investigation, this study explores barriers such as security concerns, insufficient understanding, low customer awareness, technological limitations, digital literacy challenges, budgetary constraints, and trust issues that affect their readiness to implement online payment methods.

Background of the Study

Digital Payment Systems
Local Sari-Sari Stores

The local government of Malabon City has officially partnered with GCash as part of their modernization of financial transactions to allow cashless payments and increase the availability of electronic money services for people and companies. Although these measures have been taken, uneven digital payment technology adoption continues to be a situation with many traditional businesses still sticking to cash-based methods.

This reluctance stems from various barriers including technical difficulty, inadequate infrastructure, anxiety regarding security, and digital literacy challenges. Numerous studies have analyzed the determinants of electronic payment adoption in sari-sari shops, but most of them are conducted in places such as Davao City, whose tech support and buyer attitude may vary from other localities.

Problem Statement

While the availability of cashless payment systems is continually on the rise in the Philippines, many sari-sari store proprietors in Malabon City prefer to transact in cash, mainly due to a lack of trust, limited knowledge, and security and cost concerns. This hesitancy not only constricts business operations' efficiency but also impedes the widespread adoption of cashless payment methods that the local government is promoting.

Limited research has explored how urban density, diverse customer demographics, and varying levels of digital literacy affect e-payment adoption in small retail businesses in Malabon City. This gap highlights the need for localized studies that examine operational, technological, and transaction-related factors, providing insights that are directly relevant to policymakers, financial institutions, and sari-sari store owners in the city.

Introduction

The increasing adoption of technology has changed how numerous companies manage financial transactions. Online payment platforms, including wallets, internet banking and QR code payments provide convenience, quickness and safety for both businesses and their customers. Due to these advantages government bodies and financial institutions in the Philippines have promoted payment options, for micro and small business operators.

Nonetheless with these improvements numerous sari-sari shop proprietors still depend on conventional cash dealings. Sari-sari stores continue to be a component of local neighborhoods particularly in urban locations such as Malabon City, where they function as convenient suppliers of everyday essentials. Research indicates that although certain sari-sari stores have started using wallets many store owners continue to encounter obstacles, like security worries, insufficient understanding, low customer awareness and technological limitations which affect their readiness to implement online payment methods.

Many sari-sari store owners still use cash despite the advantages of online payment systems because of issues such low digital literacy, budgetary limitations, outdated technology, and worries about security and trust. These problems emphasize the necessity of investigating the reasons behind their non-adoption.
